What is an everpark?

the regular snowpark versus the everpark
An everpark is different than the regular snowpark in a few ways. The idea is basically the same, but the roots are completely different.
As you know, almost all snowparks are located within ski resorts. Ski Resorts these days seem to be all about making suitcases full of cash and building five star hotels and restaurants to make even more.
What about their snowriding roots? It seems like they're taking the 'ski' out of 'ski resort.' The average price of a day pass today is about $52.00! The snowriding youth of today can't afford that! The lines are usually long, the slopes are crowded, the terrain is neglected, the prices of food and services are high, and there's a lot of corporate politics going into the whole process. It's not about the people anymore, it's about money.
This is where everpark comes in. everparks are built by eveRide, a non-profit organization founded by a rag-tag team of snowboarder youth. The prices at an everpark will be less than half of what most resorts charge. eveRide won't be making resorts, restaurants, or hotels. We'd rather appeal to all the poor snowriding punks out there that just want to ride.

The everpark Revolution

revolutions in riding
everpark is dedicated to giving you the best riding experience ever. New ideas to make riding safer, bigger, and better are constantly coming to the surface. There are dozens of advantages to riding at a small specialized park. We will specialize in every kind of extreme terrain. We will have gentle slopes and low challenges for beginner riders, a city section with rails, tables, and everything else imaginable (and some not so imaginable) to session on, a pipe section with a plethora of transferable quarter pipes and half pipes. We'll have a big air section, with every kind of tabletop, kicker, and jump that can be built. You will even see a section dedicated entirely to sledding and tubing at everpark. It will be my dream coming true. I hope you are just as stoked as I am
revolutions in terrain
everpark will have amazing terrain combinations you've only seen in dreams, video games, and really cool movies. Technical combinations of rails, picnic tables, jumps, benches, cars, walls, drops, boxes, and even some top secret stuff will grace our slopes and push your riding skills to the limit. If you have a wild idea or an interesting suggestion about new terrain please leave it on the Terrain Design page. Chances are, we'll end up creating it for you.
revolutions in quality
everpark is dedicated to making things absolutely perfect. I wouldn't want it any other way. Our runs will always be groomed to perfection. Our jumps will always be level and predictable. Our rails will always be slick and stable. Our pipes will never be choppy or uneven. How can we do that? We're going to hire a team of skilled riders called white glove groomers. Their job will be nothing but ride, have fun, make customers feel comfortable, and fix any kind of problem with any of our terrain before you even know it exists.
revolutions in safety
Besides having a fun time, safety is the greatest concern at everpark. Safety and fun go hand in hand, because you can't have fun if you're sitting in a body-cast sipping turkey from a straw. Our white glove groomers will always be making sure there aren't any bogus dangers to cripple anybody lying around the everparks, and if somebody does get hurt they'll be right there to help. Our air terrain will be built to comply with the laws of physics to give you a softer landing at the right time. You will never land flat. Any protruding poles or obstacles will be piped instead of padded. The piping will either allow you to vert off or up the pole for an extra feature, or the slope of the piping will slow you down if you're out of control. Powerful lights for night riding will be hung on wires instead of atop poles that get in your way. Riding at night might even be brighter than riding in the daytime. New lifts will be safer and more comfortable. everpark will even be adding top secret equipment around the resort to reduce all kinds of accidents.
revolutions in pricing
I know what it's like to be ripped off by a ski resort. Laying down three Jacksons for a single day of riding crappy terrain is not my idea of a good deal. Neither is emptying the remaining bills from your pockets for a cold piece of pizza or soggy hamburger. everpark is not into building hotels, condos, or ritzy restaurants. The entrance fees will be as low as possible, and will support maintenance, employee salaries, and improvements to the park. Entrance fees will easily be less than half of the ridiculous ski-resort average of $52.00. Food won't cost you your left arm either.
I don't like the idea of paying a ton of money to get locked into a season pass either. The times I've done that the ski-resort closed at the peak base of the year, not to mention it barely snowed that year. Well, there's a better way to save you money and keep you from getting ripped off by corporate schemes and nature. We have it, you'll see it, and we'll all dig it. Get ready for the everpass.
revolutions in music
We're hoping to construct a park-wide jukebox so you can listen to your favorite tunes on high powered speakers while you ride. everpark also plans on building a large amphitheater in most parks, so your favorite bands will be able to play for you while you ride, and their tunes will be amped all over the park for your personal listening enjoyment.

The Anti-Lift Revolution

what is the anti-lift revolution?
Right now you are probably thinking, "Why would anybody be anti-lift?" Don't get me wrong, lifts are good! However, if you'll pardon this awesome pun, the average ski-lift is about to get a face-lift. Good one eh?

If you think about it, lifts are pretty lame. We just keep them because nothing better has come along. Have you ever been stuck on a lift on a really cold day? What about really long lift lines? Have you ever hiked a rail or something because that's all you wanted to work on? Have you ever been tripped up while getting on or off a lift? Do your legs ever get sore while riding them? Have you ever fallen from a lift? Lifts are really expensive to operate. And what about the environmental damage?

Well, it's time for all that to change.
I can't tell you how it's done, but these "anti-lifts" are going to be dope. You can get on and off without removing any bindings whenever you want, they're totally comfortable, totally safe, and 9 out of 10 babes approve. They're less expensive to operate (which will keep the prices lower), they will move more people up the hill with smaller lines, and they won't scar the environment. The anti-lift is just one more thing that will keep the everparks a step ahead.

White Glove Groomers

the ultimate groomer
White Glove Groomers will be the backbone of our terrain, and lengthen the gap between everparks and the average snowpark. They will build the terrain to meet our lofty standards of safety, test it, and then ride the park throughout the day making sure it stays in perfect condition. Another goal is to have them complete the re-building and repairing of terrain in the shortest time possible, so there won't be a lot of closed features.
Not only will the white gloves groom the park, but they'll also look after the riders. Their job will be to make it the best possible experience for you. If you tell them to fix something or build something, they'll do it. If you want them to give you some advice, they'll give it. If they see you bail hard, they'll help you up (or get the medic!) If you are a beginner they'll help you one-on-one.

what is "city terrain"?
City terrain is anything you might find in... well... a city. Perhaps a better name for this section of everpark would be the technical terrain section. This type of terrain includes rails, picnic tables, boxes, and other unique terrain you've never seen before. Some of it is SO unique, it's classified until we can build it.
the cut of our jib
At everpark, we don't know why rails feel so good or look so sick to ride, we just know they do. everpark is going to have a whole plethora of rails. We plan to have more types of rails than most other snowparks have rails. From the typical strait rail, to kinks, to curves, to short, to long, to high, to low, to ones that you won't even know how to ride until you get a little creative. Give us an idea for a rail or box in the Terrain Design page and we'll probably weld it up for you. Also, don't forget you can look at the concept drawings for most of our unclassified rails on our Terrain Graphics Page.
unique technical features
We're going to be a little more creative at everpark. Have you ever wanted to grind a car? A picnic table? A garbage can? A rooftop? How about airing onto a semi truck trailer? I have, and once this place is built I'm going to. We're going to put unique combinations of this kind of terrain so you can enjoy a nice city-like grind session. We want you to get creative with your riding. Got anything else you want to grind? Go ahead and let us know what it is on the Terrain Design page.
choice maintenance
As you've probably guessed we really love to ride. However, there are times when we don't enjoy it as much as we could. Poorly built takeoffs, loose rails, chunks of icy snow, and bad planning can easily make for a bogus ride (sometimes ending in a red and white helecopter). We have a solution. Our White Glove Groomers will maintain all our technical features throughout the day and make sure they're safe, smooth, and stable.
TOP SECRET TERRAIN
I really wish I could tell you more about the top secret terrain we're going to have at everpark. These features will be unique and will only be at the everparks. This special terrain will honestly revolutionize winter riding sports. Some of the top secret terrain will be quite basic, something you'd see and say, "Why didn't I think of that?" However, some of the terrain will be so off-the-wall you won't know what it's for. Nobody's going to tell you how to ride it... you'll just have to get creative and see what can be done with it. Trust me on this one folks, when you ride it, you're going to love it. Names you can expect to hear in the future will be the techflex, dinoback, loaf, split, and "YR" rails.

See Air Terrain Sketches

big air
everpark will be THE big air location. We plan to dedicate almost half of our park to big air terrain, so there will always be enough room to soar. We hope to have every kind of jump, from table tops to canyon gaps to cheese-wedge kickers. We hope to have a large enough area so there aren't lines or crowds waiting to drop in that you might expect at regular resorts.
safety philosophies
There is nothing worse than landing too short or too far. There have been times when I have soared over a table top, thinking the landing would be much longer for the takeoff provided, and cleared the entire thing, landing on my feet and crumpling because it was about a 30 foot drop to flat ground from the peak of my air. The day before this happened the table top was long enough. I compressed my spine and was off the slopes for a week. And there have been countless times I've gone off a table top that started out nice, but by the end of the day there was a huge hole or worn down spot where everybody had been landing. The 'slope' was practically flat. Poorly made jumps are accidents waiting to happen. A jump is a clear indication of how a resort cares about the people that ride here.
attention to detail
Poorly built jumps will not exist at everpark. When our jumps are first built, we're going to measure things out, test things out, and make sure the air is safe. Designated starting points will be made so you know where to start your run at the jump. This will allow riders to start at the correct place, so they never fly too far, and never land short unless they slow down significantly. Jumps at everpark will always take off and land on the correct slope. Landing on a flat surface is NEVER going to happen if we can help it. If there is ever any problem with a jump, our White Glove Groomers will gladly fix it for you if they haven't corrected the problem already.
TOP SECRET
everpark is going to patent and install one of the most revolutionary safety devices on every jump. The device will show anybody above the jump if the landing area is clear or not.

See Pipe Terrain Sketches

the basic pipe
A 'pipe' or 'vert' can come in many different forms. Full pipes, half pipes, and quarter pipes. We're going to have them all.
Grade 'A' quality
Many ski resorts have pipe terrain, yet so few resorts have good pipe terrain. Most pipes are maintained at a B- level. They are choppy, unkempt, and unsafe. At everparks, the pipe terrain will always be A grade due to the staff of white glove groomers who do nothing but ride and groom. If you ever have a problem with any of the pipes, tell the groomers and they'll gladly fix it for you.
half pipes
everparks is planning on having three joined half pipes, and a few independent half pipes. The three joined half pipes will allow riders to transfer from pipe to pipe, if they wish, and keep each pipe from getting crowded. Independent pipes will vary in height and have specialties such as coping, raised boxes, rails, height meters, and picnic tables to jib.
quarter pipes
Quarter pipes are going to be all over the place. Some quarter pipes will have coping, tables, and other fun stuff at the top. Some quarter pipes will transfer to and out of other pipes. Quarter pipes will vary in height, and will always be well maintained. Question: Is everparks going to have one of those insanely huge quarter pipes? Answer: yup.
safety pipes
Instead of having all kinds of poles in the way for lights and lifts, we figure we might as well make them into something fun. We are going to 'pipe' every pole that sticks out of the ground to make things safer, and so you can session things that otherwise wouldn't be sessionable. One of the coolest things I've seen is when I saw some guy ride up a bank and stalled a post sticking out of the ground. So it wasn't the most technical thing ever, but the guy was creative. If you're that guy, rock on!

slope style
If you're like I am, you love a run that has a little of everything in it. So you know exactly what I mean when I say that if the everpark slopestyle course will be a cornucopia of snowriding goodness, which happens to be filled with plenty of wholesome home-cooked lovin' straight from the oven of old Granny Shred. (Or something like that.) The slopestyle course will be littered with the standard rails, boxes, picnic tables, tabletops, quarterpipes, and even a few classified safety features. Of course it will also be groomed and maintained with as much care as the rest of the park by our white glove groomers.

being new
Trying something new is a tough experience. It's hard to get out of the comfort zone, especially at the risk of falling on your face and looking like an idiot. My first times snowboarding were easy. It was just me, my brother, and a few of his friends. Yeah, if I wrecked, they'd beat me up and laugh and what not, but that was just my brother... he'd beat me up for anything.
beginners have the right
Something I don't like is when I see new kids out there on the slopes giving it their all, and some "tuff shred" rips down the hill, cuts them off, and gives them the finger. No matter what the skill level, everybody has a right to be on the hill having fun. It's all about learning. Everybody that rides, whether they're pros or first-timers, are always learning.
experienced riders have the right
Another annoyance is when a rider is heading towards a big hit and a cute little kid in a puffy coat rides up the front of the kicker, then either slides back down or falls off the end. Not only is it dangerous for this little guy, but its dangerous to the rider as well. Two people lose in this situation. The advanced rider doesn't get to ride his favorite jump, and the little guy is in obvious danger.
the solution?
If you are a beginner, wouldn't it be comfortable to ride somewhere where you're not going to be cut off, or mocked, or laughed at, where everybody's falling on their face? Somewhere where you can try new things, without hitting the big jumps, the long rails, the high pipes? Maybe just a nice hill to learn how to turn? If you are an advanced rider, wouldn't it be nice never to be cut off by that little puffy coat? And if you are a parent, would you feel comfortable having your kids away from the "tuff shreds" in an area where they won't kill themselves?
good times to be had by all
By having a section of the park dedicated to beginner terrain, all riders would have an excellent time. Advanced riders would enjoy advanced terrain, while beginners would enjoy beginner terrain. Makes sense, eh?
beginner terrain features
The beginner section would have a sizeable hill on a gentle slope where new riders could relax and learn the basics. This hill would have no obstacles or specialized terrain. There would also be a slightly more advanced area in the section. This area would have low rails, low jumps, a low half pipe, low quarter pipes, and other beginner-friendly features. We'll even have a device that won't let beginners fall. How? You'll find out.
helping you improve
Need some help? Sometimes it's nice to have a coach, and sometimes it's nice to try it for yourself. Just in case you want some advice, some tips, or a pal on the slopes, our White Glove Groomers will be there to help you out.

Current Projections

here's the latest news
For now, the projections easily aren't set in stone. At the moment, research is being done on a few different locations for the first everpark. The most promising location looks to be in Utah, the center of snowriding in the U.S. All you need to know is that it's within an hour south of the Salt Lake City Airport, Park City, and the home of the 2002 Olympic Winter Games. Needless to say it gets plenty of the Greatest Snow on Earth. As more studies are done, we'll get more specific on where the first everpark will be built. For now we're putting together a little beta project for this winter. It's invite-only, and it's not going to be anything huge... but it's a start. We'll have pictures up this winter.
